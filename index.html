What is DTOAS?

  DeLong Traffic Offender Assessment Services (DTOAS) is a state-certified Offender Management Unit (OMU) which offers screening assessments (Driver Risk Inventory Screening) to determine the level of services needed for SATOP participants. We offer Assessment Services, Offender Education Program (OEP) and Weekend Intervention Program (WIP) level programs. We schedule the OEP the first weekend of the month and the WIP the third weekend of the month unless holidays interfere. 

  We are very flexible and will work with you! We offer services on an as-needed basis, and are willing to provide services day or night, 7 days a week.




The SATOP Assessment/Screening Process


  • The first thing you must do is call to make an appointment for the assessment screening.

  • The assessment process takes about one hour and involves completing a 113-item questionnaire/test, made up of mostly true/false and multiple choice questions.

  • After completing the test, your answers are entered into a computer program and results are printed instantly. The program has standard guidelines that have been implemented into the SATOP process that require a person to attend certain programs if certain criteria are present. These criteria are taken into consideration at the time of the assessment.

  • We will meet and discuss the results of the test, gather other information, and then based on all information available, you will be referred to one of the SATOP programs/classes listed below that you must successfully complete.
